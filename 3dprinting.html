My 3D Printing Journey

During the pandemic, healthcare workers were struggling to gain access to personal protective equipment (PPE), so I started handmaking face shields and donating them to local hospitals. To automate the process, I invested in a 3D printer and continued donating face shields. Then, I started selling PPE at a low and affordable price. 3D printing grabbed my attention, and I couldn't stop printing. Below is a gallery of some of my favorite prints, enjoy!

Gallery

Faceshields

This was one of the first things I've ever printed!

MaskFitters

Here's another example of a PPE item. This object is contoured to your face and goes over a surgical mask to provide a tighter fit (equivalent to a N95 and no more foggy glasses!).

Ear Savers

After wearing a mask for hours, my ears hurt and started turning red! This device takes the pressure off of our ears by shifting the elastic down to your neck and having the tension on the device (hence the name ear savers). We made a special "HERO" edition to gift to friends, family, and your HERO!

Mom's Cafe Sign (Mother's Day Gift)

My mom loves to cook! Every weekend she sends me new recipes, excited to try them. My dad and I worked together to make this sign. All of the letters you see are 3D printed and the base is wood. Happy Mother's Day Mom!

Kriya Yoga Sign

My local meditation center, Kriya Yoga, was looking for a sign to identify a residential house where guests can live when visiting. My father and I volunteered, and we 3D printed this sign and used wood as our base. They loved it!

Airpods Case

My sister is very clumsy with her Airpods, and we hate whenever they drop and they scatter all over the floor. I designed this minimalistic colorful case to protect our Airpods!
